    Adult social care

    The provision of support services for adults with physical or mental health needs, and their carers.

    About this area

    Adult social care sits in the Business Areas layer of the Local Government Architecture Model. It covers the statutory duties that councils perform under the Care Act 2014 — from assessing need and determining eligibility through to arranging care, managing safeguarding, and shaping the local care market.

    The functions within this area are defined by the LGA taxonomy under "Adult services" (function 59). Each function contains a set of granular services that councils deliver.

    Related residential and hospice care services are covered under Care homes, supported and sheltered housing (function 60), which spans both adult and children's domains.

    Capabilities

    Capabilities from the LGAM Capabilities layer that support adult social care services.

    • Workflow — case management through referral, assessment, care planning, and review stages
    • Forms — online referrals, self-assessments, financial assessment forms
    • Payments — direct payments, client contributions, provider payments
    • Identity — person verification for safeguarding and shared records
    • Booking — day care sessions, respite slots, occupational therapy appointments

    Key data sources

    National data collections, registers, and surveys relevant to adult social care technology. Sources are drawn from the Catalogue of Social Care Data, filtered to official UK public sector publications.

    Registers and APIs

    • CQC Provider Register — national register of all regulated care providers (care homes, home care agencies, supported living). Includes inspection ratings, location data, and registration categories. Available via REST API.
    • LG Inform — benchmarking metrics for adult social care performance, drawn from ASC-CLD and ASCOF data.

    Statutory collections

    • ASC-CLD (Adult Social Care Client Level Data) — individual-level data collection that replaced SALT. Submitted by all councils covering referrals, assessments, services provided, and outcomes. Defines the core data items that case management systems must capture.
    • ASC-FR (Adult Social Care Finance Return) — annual expenditure data by service type, client group, and care setting.
    • SAE (Safeguarding Adults, England) — local authority data on safeguarding enquiries, types of abuse, risk sources, and outcomes.
    • DoLS (Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards) — data on applications, authorisations, and outcomes under the Mental Capacity Act 2005.
    • Deferred Payment Agreements — data on local authority DPA loans for people whose care costs are secured against their property.

    Surveys

    • ASCS (Adult Social Care Survey) — annual survey of service users on quality of life, satisfaction with services, and control over daily life.
    • SACE (Survey of Adult Carers in England) — biennial survey of unpaid carers on quality of life, work-life balance, and support received.

    Workforce data

    • ASC-WDS (Adult Social Care Workforce Data Set) — the leading source of workforce intelligence, covering recruitment, retention, pay, qualifications, and vacancy rates. Maintained by Skills for Care.

    Regulator

    • CQC (Care Quality Commission) — the independent regulator of health and adult social care in England. Inspects and rates providers, publishes enforcement action, and maintains the provider register used for commissioning.

    Standards

    • PRSB (Professional Record Standards Body) — develops information standards for health and social care records, including the social care core content standard that defines what should be in a shared care record.

    Sector bodies

    • ADASS (Association of Directors of Adult Social Services) — the professional leadership body for directors of adult social services in English local authorities.
    • Think Local Act Personal — a national partnership promoting personalisation, community-based support, and outcomes-focused practice.
    Unlike the planning domain, there is no single national open data platform for adult social care. Data about individuals is sensitive and not openly published. The key integration points for council systems are the CQC provider register (for commissioning) and the ASC-CLD data specification (which defines what case management systems must record).

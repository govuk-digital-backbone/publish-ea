Intro

The local government architecture model (LGAM) is designed to be a guide to the public technology layers that are used to deliver local public services.

The LGAM is part of a wider vision for local government technology. It guides:

  • the Government Digital Service (GDS) in building common products, including those for local government
  • government departments that build products and services for councils
  • councils who want to work with other local authorities on products and services
  • vendors who need to make common capabilities, patterns and dependencies more visible

The LGAM is a work in progress and will continue to evolve as we work alongside local government to refine it.

For more information on how the LGAM was created, read our blogs on:

  • sourcing the stack for local government technology
  • an understanding of technical architecture in local government
  • how we arrived at the first iteration of the model

Public Channels

Public channels are how service users interact with councils, so government services are required to be delivered across as many channels as possible. The Service Manual provides more information in point 3: provide a joined up experience across all channels.

In person
Some council services will still need to be delivered in-person. This includes at a council’s offices, at a household or business, or at another location.
Phone
Some services are delivered via telephone, allowing users to speak directly with council staff for support, enquiries, or transactions where digital access may not be suitable/available to the user.
Email
Asynchronous written communication via email.
Letter
Some services rely on postal communication, particularly for formal notifications, legal correspondence or where physical record of delivery is required.
Online
Many council services are delivered online through websites, portals or forms, enabling users to self-serve at a time that suits them.
SMS/ Messaging
Some services may use SMS or messaging channels to provide updates, reminders or quick interactions.
Social Media
Some councils may use social media platforms to share information, respond to enquiries and engage with residents in a more informal/accessible way.
Smart Device
Some services can be accessed through smart devices such as Internet of Things, to enable automated interaction where appropriate.
Third Party
Users may sometimes access council services through proxy; by going through their local MP or local media.
Video
Some councils may use video calls to deliver service remotely, allowing users to speak face-to-face with staff without needing to attend in person.
Council Agents

Agents are interfaces which allow service users to contact and use a council’s services.

App
Native or cross-platform mobile application providing dedicated services to citizens.
API Gateway
Machine-to-machine interface handling third-party integrations and smart device inputs.
Automated Phone
Automated telephony system, such as IVR or conversational voicebots.
Human
Customer Service Representatives or specialist officers handling queries.
Public Device
These are devices owned by the council that are in the public domain, and include technology such as touch-screens in a public location for example in a library, or an internet of things device for example in a drain or bin.
Website
The primary council web portal, forms, and digital content interfaces.
Capabilities

Capabilities are tools that council agents use to meet the needs of their service users.

Agentic AI
Autonomous AI agents capable of reasoning, executing multi-step tasks, and making routing decisions.
Booking
Councils use booking tools to arrange and manage appointments or service slots, such as inspections, and interviews with users.
Email
Emails are used as the primary tool to send and receive messages when dealing with users, internal/external stakeholders.
Forms
Forms are used to collect and check information provided by users, such as in applications, reports and requests.
Identity
Councils use identity tools to check and verify who a user is.
Payments
Payment tools are used to take and track payments made by users for services, such as council tax, licences or permits.
SMS
Councils use SMS tools to send and receive text messages for updates, reminders and quick communications with users.
Telephony / Fax
Councils use telephone (and in some cases where still needed, fax) to communicate/exchange information with their users.
Workflow
These are used to manage and track users request/application/casework as it moves through different processing stages.
Business Areas

Business areas cover the specific functions each council must provide. Each features individual technology components designed for that specific need.

Adult Social Care
Support services for adults with physical or mental health needs and their carers.
Children’s Social Care
This includes services such as child protection and safeguarding, children in need and family support and care for looked-after children & care leavers.
Democratic Services
Management of elections, committee meetings, and support for elected councillors.
Education
School admissions, transport, special educational needs, and support for local educational settings.
Highways / Transport
Road maintenance, traffic management, public transport planning, and parking.
Housing
Management of social housing, repairs, and homelessness prevention.
Leisure / Culture
Operation of parks, libraries, museums, leisure centres, and community events.
Licencing / Regulation
Processing of alcohol, taxi, and trade licences, and environmental health enforcement.
Planning / Development
Processing of planning applications, building control, and local spatial planning.
Public Health
Community health initiatives, sexual health services, and health protection.
Revenues / Benefits
Collection of Council Tax and Business Rates, and administration of Housing Benefit.
Waste Management
Kerbside collections, recycling, and operation of household waste centres.
Corporate Areas

Corporate areas are business areas that are not specific to councils, each with their own individual technology components.

Business Planning / Performance
Strategic planning, performance monitoring, and business intelligence.
Communications / PR
Management of press relations and public campaigns. Capabilities include Omnichannel Content Publishing.
Corporate Governance / Risk
Internal audit, risk management, and ensuring constitutional compliance.
Customer Relationship / B2C
Management of customer service centres and general resident engagement.
Facilities
Maintenance and management of council-owned buildings and estates.
Financial
Accounting, treasury management, payroll, and budget setting.
Geographical
Management of GIS data, mapping services, and gazetteers.
Legal / Compliance
Provision of legal advice, litigation services, and data protection (GDPR).
Human Resource / Workforce
Recruitment, staff training, performance management, and employee relations.
Procurement / B2B
Management of contracts, supply chains, and purchasing goods and services.

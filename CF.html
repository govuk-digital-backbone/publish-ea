Introduction

The Citizen-Facing Architecture provides a clear, logical, layered view of how UK government digital services interact within a complex environment. Each layer represents a distinct function, from citizen-facing channels to data and infrastructure, and shows how these layers work together to deliver secure, consistent, and accessible experiences. While the actual topology of government systems is far more complex, this architecture offers a simplified version to help users understand the key components and their relationships. It brings together operational services and planned innovations under one framework, simplifying access to information and helping teams design services that work across departments. Who this is for

Who this is for

This view is designed for:

  • Service Designers planning future digital services.
  • Developers & Architects integrating with public sector APIs.
  • Private Sector Partners collaborating on digital solutions.
  • Citizens who want to understand how services connect.

How to use it

Use this architecture as:

  • A reference model for designing citizen-facing services.
  • A guide for aligning new initiatives with common government digital standards and shared capabilities.
  • A tool for identifying integration points with APIs and data layers.
  • A roadmap for understanding what’s operational today and what’s planned.
